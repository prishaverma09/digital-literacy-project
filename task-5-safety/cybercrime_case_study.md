Task 5 Part A: Cybercrime Case Study. Phishing

 1. Overview of the Phishing threat

Phishing is a kind of attack where bad people pretend to be someone you trust like your bank or college to get your information such as your login details, credit card numbers or personal identity. For a student like me this can look like an email from "College Admin" asking to verify my password. The Phishing attack is very tricky. Can fool anyone.

  2. Real-World Impact

Many students at universities have been targeted by Phishing attacks in recent years. The attackers send job offer letters to students and this leads to both financial loss and identity theft. The students lose their money. The attackers get access to their academic and personal documents. The Phishing attack is a problem for students.

  3. Legal Framework: IT Act, 2000

In India the Phishing crimes are governed by the Information Technology Act, 2000.

 Section 66C of the IT Act 2000 deals with punishment for identity theft, which includes the use of electronic signatures or passwords.

 Section 66D of the IT Act 2000 covers punishment for cheating by personation by using computer resources, which's the main law used to prosecute the people who do Phishing attacks.

  4. Prevention Strategy

To prevent Phishing attacks I use Multi-Factor Authentication. Always check the senders email address domain before clicking any links. I also use the "Report Phishing" tools in my college email to tell the IT department, about activity. This way I can protect myself from Phishing attacks.
